THE HISTORY OF PHOTOGRAPHY

Photography, as we know it, has come to be a form of art. This artwork can be done when the object being photographed is either "in-motion" or "static". According to history, Joseph Nicéphore Niépce is the first man ever to take a photograph, better known as, "The Founder of Photography". Despite the fact that Joseph's works were lost in time, his title still remains strong.

Looking deeper into history, historians made a discovery that in 1717, Johann Heinrich Schulze used a light sensitive slurry to capture images of writings in a bottle. Despite that,he did not will to make his photographic results permanent. Instead, in 1800, Thomas Wedgwood made unsuccessful attempts in making photographic results permanent together with his associate, Humphry Davy. It was not until 1826 that Nicéphore Niépce managed to fix an image captured with a camera after exposing the camera for several days or at least 8 hours.
Afterwards, Louis Daguerre invented the daguerreotype process where a camera required few minutes of exposure in order to acquire plausible results. (Sourced from Wikipedia)

Fast-forward to the present, photography has become more of an art rather than just a discovery for the market worldwide. Now, photography has become more of talent enjoyed by many as much as people enjoy seeing the work of William Shakespeare and that of Van Gogh.
